~Billy the Kid (November 23, 1859 – July 14, 1881) Billy the Kid was born Henry McCarty on November 23, 1859. From a young age, he looked different from other boys. He was tall, with blue eyes and smooth skin, and he wore a simple Mexican sombrero. People who met him said he was polite and friendly. He seemed honest and ready to work. But even as a child, he could not stay out of trouble. Some say he read stories about outlaws and wanted to live like them. His first arrest was in April 1875 for stealing cheese. Later that year, he was caught again, but this time he escaped by climbing up a chimney. From that moment, he became a fugitive. Billy traveled to New Mexico. He worked as a cowboy, but he also stole cattle. Violence was common there. One rancher, John Tunstall, was killed by men connected to local merchants. Billy wanted revenge. He joined a gang of outlaws, men who promised to fight for him. The gang stole cattle and moved through towns quickly, always trying to stay ahead of the law. On December 23, 1880, the police found them at a place called Stinking Springs. The gang was sleeping in an abandoned building. The lawmen waited until morning. When the smell of breakfast came from the fire, the officers called the men to come out and eat. The outlaws had no choice. They gave up and shared the meal. Billy’s trouble with the law did not end. On April 13, 1881, he was sentenced to hang. But Billy was clever. On April 28, while guarded by two deputies, he killed both men and escaped. People were amazed at how he got away. He stayed free for a few more months. Then, on July 14, 1881, his luck ran out. Sheriff Pat Garrett went to the house of Billy’s friend. Around midnight, Billy came inside. It was dark, and he did not see Garrett. When he raised his gun, Garrett fired two shots. One bullet hit Billy above his heart. He died instantly. He was only twenty-one years old. Even though his life was short, the stories about Billy the Kid lasted for generations. People still remember him as a young, clever outlaw who could not be caught.
