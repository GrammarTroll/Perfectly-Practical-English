Paragraph 1 – Introduction Passage 1: Setting Purpose: Establish time and place Grammar focus includes: Adverbs of time Prepositions of time Adverbs of frequency Past simple Passage 2: Characters Purpose: Introduce who is involved Grammar focus includes: Articles and determiners There is / There are Relative clauses Present simple Adverbs of frequency Comparatives Demonstratives Paragraph 2 – Conflict Development Passage 3: Problem Purpose: Introduce the central conflict Grammar focus includes: Past continuous Adverbs of manner Prepositions When / While clauses Past perfect Gerunds and infinitives Passage 4: Rising Action Purpose: Show events leading toward conflict Grammar focus includes: Present perfect continuous Present perfect Past simple Infinitives Paragraph 3 – Interaction Passage 5: Dialogue Purpose: Reveal character intentions and conflict Grammar focus includes: Reported speech Possessives Questions Question tags Passage 6: Persuasion Purpose: One character attempts to influence another Grammar focus includes: Present continuous Modals of ability and requests Reported speech (commands/requests) Paragraph 4 – Intention and Motivation Passage 7: Plans / Future Purpose: Show decisions and intentions Grammar focus includes: Future with will and going to Reported future forms Indirect questions Passage 8: Motivation Purpose: Explain desires, needs, and reasoning Grammar focus includes: Would like / Would rather Modals of possibility Modals of obligation, necessity Paragraph 5 – Resolution Peak Passage 9: Climax Purpose: The turning point of the story Grammar focus includes: Reported speech Exclamations “I wish” constructions Conjunctions Paragraph 6 – Lesson Passage 10: Moral Purpose: State the lesson or truth of the fable Grammar focus includes: Conditional sentences Imperatives How to Use This as a Teaching Key Before reading: Students predict grammar based on passage type (e.g., “Dialogue = reported speech”).
