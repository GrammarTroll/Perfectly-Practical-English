~Past Simple World of Dinosaurs Today we will venture back in time to a world of prehistoric monsters. We will look at some of the biggest, strangest, strongest, fastest and dumbest creatures that ever walked the Earth. One of the best ways to talk about prehistoric creatures is to use past simple tense since most creatures from the Earth’s distant past are extinct. They are dead and gone. Just as there is much to learn about dinosaurs, there is also much to learn about the past simple tense. For example, there are regular and irregular verbs. Regular verbs are easy – you add an “-ed” sound to the end of regular verbs to express the past form. For example, the past form of “walk” is “walked”. But to express the past form of irregular verbs, you’ll have to learn the unique spelling for each irregular verb. For example, the past form of “run” is “ran”. Now, some teachers teach regular and irregular verbs separately. But regular and irregular verbs are found everywhere “in the wild ,” and you can’t speak English well without using both kinds of verbs. So now, prepare to enter… And brace yourself as we explore the frightening world of regular and irregular verb forms alongside the fascinating world of prehistoric creatures. The time was about 65 million years ago. It was a dangerous world. It was a tropical world where it rained every day. Lightning flashed and thunder rumbled. Hot mud bubbled. Volcanoes erupted and lava oozed all over the place. And dinosaurs ruled the Earth. All kinds of weird, exotic and dangerous creatures walked about. Dinosaurs dominated the steamy jungles and swam in the warm seas. Pterosaurs flew in the skies overhead and soared over the jungle landscape below. They glided on high and then descended on their prey. They returned to their nests and fed their hatchlings with the poor helpless creatures that they had snatched up. The enormous brontosaurus inhabited the swamp. He stretched out his long neck and browsed for sustenance in the tall trees. He dined on moss and leaves and grew so heavy that the ground trembled and the trees shook when he walked. As he moved slowly about and nibbled a little of this and a little of that, he kept watch over the swamp. If a predator appeared, he waded deeper into the swamp. He retreated into the lake and escaped. Meanwhile the Stegosaurus wandered around and grazed like a cow and lapped water from the streams. He was protected by massive armor plates, and he had boney spikes on his tail, but he avoided trouble if possible. He was always worried about danger, and looked around nervously as he searched for the tasty morsels that he loved. He grunted like a pig as he feasted on prehistoric vegetation, but if he heard the approach of the Tyrannosaurus, he squealed and fled. All the forest animals feared the Tyrannosaurus. The carnivorous Tyrannosaurus preyed upon other dinosaurs. He wanted meat, and when his stomach rumbled, he hunted. There was nothing he liked better than to eat a fat Stegosaurus. Whenever he howled and growled, he was heard for miles. The startled birds of the jungle warned of his approach, and all the forest animals scurried away and hid themselves. The Tyrannosaurus was built for hunting. He was 12 meters long and stood 5 meters tall. He pursued his prey on powerful legs. He jumped on them and shredded them with his sharp claws, and he gnashed his enormous teeth as he grabbed them up and devoured them. He used his claws and teeth as mighty instruments of destruction. He tore them to pieces, chewed them up and swallowed them. He drank their blood and then licked his lips and roared. Then he slept as he digested his meal. One day the Tyrannosaurus attacked a Triceratops as it guarded a nest of dinosaur eggs. The two dinosaurs prepared for epic combat and fought each other. The Tyrannosaurus clawed and bit the Triceratops. The Tyrannosaurus now tasted blood and desired to gorge himself on the delicious Triceratops flesh. The Triceratops gored the Tyrannosaurus with his horns. Injured, the Tyrannosaurus bellowed. The two dinosaurs battled for hours until the Triceratops outsmarted the Tyrannosaurus. The Triceratops tricked the Tyrannosaurus and lured him near a tar pit. The Tyrannosaurus followed the Triceratops and stepped closer and closer. Then the Triceratops pushed him in. The Tyrannosaurus fell into the sticky tar and was trapped forever. The Triceratops had won. And that is how dinosaurs flourished 65 million years ago. Until a meteor streaked across the sky and fell into the great dinosaur swamp. It crashed deep into the marsh and struck the tectonic plates with such force when it landed that it exploded and destroyed the entire world of dinosaurs. It impacted the whole ecosystem and changed history forever. After the meteor hit the planet, all the dinosaurs became extinct. They were all killed and none of them survived. Dinosaurs once ruled the earth, but they all died out. The dinosaurs believed that their world would last forever. But with that cataclysmic event, the age of dinosaurs had ended. None of the dinosaurs ever thought that it would end that way. The dinosaurs disappeared, and for millions of years, no one knew that they had ever even existed. But millions of years later, erosion uncovered the remains, and people discovered the bones of the dinosaurs. Paleontologists found their remains and dug them up. The paleontologists unearthed the dinosaur fossils, reassembled them, glued them together and placed them in museums.
